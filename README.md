<h2>Creation Steps</h2>

<img width="437" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4c4f6e0f-6ed4-40ae-b506-36f9b21a1e8e" />
</p>
<p>
1. Search and click on "Resource Groups" in Azure, then on "+Create." Name your Resource group and remember the region you choose. And click "Create" again.
  
<p>
  <img width="467" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/102b664b-cfd5-4249-99c0-e3766be46284" />
</p>
<p>
2. Next create the virtual network at the same time as your virtual machine; Virtual Machines made after this can be added to this Virtual Network. To do this, search "virtual machine" and click it and "+Create" and select "azure virtual machine" in the generated dropdown. Then make sure it is in the appropriate subscription and resource group. Name your virtual machine and select a region for your virtual machine (VM). Scroll down to image and select "Windows 10 Pro" to create your Windows VM. For size, select one that has at least 2 vcpus, otherwise it may be slow (the prices listed are indicative of if the VM was perpetually left on). Open a note pad or in some way, keep track of your username and password. Then click "Next:Disks>"
</p>
